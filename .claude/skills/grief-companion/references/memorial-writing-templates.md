# Memorial Writing Templates

Structured templates for creating meaningful tributes that capture the essence of the person being remembered.

## Obituary Template (Traditional)

```markdown
# [Full Name]
## [Birth Date] - [Death Date]

[Name] of [City, State], passed away [peacefully/suddenly/after a courageous battle]
on [Date] at [Location/Hospital/Home]. [He/She/They] was [age] years old.

### Early Life
Born on [Date] in [City, State] to [Parents' Names], [Name] grew up in [Location].
[He/She/They] attended [Schools] and [early life details].

### Career & Accomplishments
[Name] worked as a [profession] for [years/company]. [Notable accomplishments].
[He/She/They] was known for [professional reputation].

### Personal Life
[Name] married [Spouse] on [Date] in [Location]. Together they [life together].
[He/She/They] was a devoted [parent/grandparent/etc.] to [children's names].

### Passions & Interests
[Name] loved [hobbies, interests, passions]. [Specific stories or details].
[He/She/They] was happiest when [doing what].

### Legacy
[Name] will be remembered for [character traits, impact on others].
[Quote from family member or the deceased].

### Survivors
[Name] is survived by [spouse], [children], [grandchildren], [siblings],
and [other family]. [He/She/They] was preceded in death by [names].

### Services
[Funeral/Memorial service] will be held on [Date] at [Time] at [Location].
[Visitation details]. [Burial/cremation details].

In lieu of flowers, donations may be made to [Charity Name] at [Address/URL].
```

## Eulogy Structure

```markdown
# Eulogy for [Name]
## Delivered by [Your Name] on [Date]

### Opening (1-2 minutes)
- Thank people for coming
- Acknowledge the difficulty and honor of speaking
- Brief statement of your relationship

### Who [Name] Was (3-4 minutes)
- Key character traits with examples
- What made them unique
- How they made people feel

### Stories (5-7 minutes)
- 2-3 specific stories that illustrate who they were
- Mix of emotional tones (funny, touching, meaningful)
- Stories others might not know

### Their Impact (2-3 minutes)
- How they changed the people around them
- Their legacy in actions, not just words
- What we learned from them

### Closing (1-2 minutes)
- Direct address to the deceased (optional)
- Final message to the gathered
- Closing thought or quote

TOTAL: 12-18 minutes (aim for 15)
```

## Gathering Stories from Others

### Questions to Ask Family/Friends
1. What's your favorite memory of [Name]?
2. What did [Name] always say?
3. How did you meet [Name]?
4. What made [Name] laugh?
5. What would [Name] want us to remember about them?
6. What's something most people don't know about [Name]?
7. How did [Name] change your life?
8. What will you miss most?

### Recording Template

```markdown
### The Story

### Key Details
- Timeframe: [When this happened]
- Location: [Where]
- Who else was involved: [Names]

### Quotes to Pull
> "[Direct quote worth highlighting]"
```

## Tone Guidelines

### What Works
- Specific details over generalities
- Stories over adjectives
- Their voice and words
- Honest acknowledgment of complexity
- Humor when appropriate
- The small things that made them THEM

### What to Avoid
- Clich√©s ("They're in a better place")
- Sanitizing who they were
- Making it about you
- Airing grievances
- Inside jokes without context
- Too much focus on how they died

## Sample Opening Lines

**For a parent:**
"My mother had a way of making you feel like the most important person in the room..."

**For a friend:**
"I met [Name] at [place/event], and my life was never the same..."

**For someone with a good sense of humor:**
"[Name] would probably be annoyed that we're all being so serious..."

**For a complicated relationship:**
"My relationship with [Name] was complex, as many real relationships are..."

**For sudden loss:**
"None of us expected to be here today..."

**For expected loss after illness:**
"We had time to say goodbye, and for that I'm grateful..."
